Post-Philmont Recovery & First Gym Session Guide

Recovery recommendations after a 7-Day, 32-Mile Philmont Trek

Can You Go to the Gym?

Yes — if you're feeling well-rested, hydrated, and not unusually sore, going to the gym around 3:00 PM on July 10 is generally reasonable.

Things Working in Your Favor

  • A 7-day, 32-mile Philmont trek is a significant endurance effort, but you've had roughly 10.5 hours of sleep, which is a good recovery start.
  • You don't report any injury, illness, dizziness, or severe fatigue.
  • You found the trek "pretty easy," suggesting your fitness level is already fairly strong.

Recommended Workout Intensity

Your first workout back should be a lighter re-entry workout, not a maximum-effort session.

For Today's Workout

  • Warm up for 5–10 minutes by walking, cycling, or rowing.
  • Reduce weights to approximately 70–80% of your normal working weight.
  • Avoid testing one-repetition maximums (1RM).
  • Avoid training to complete muscular failure.
  • Keep the workout around 45–60 minutes.
  • Drink extra water throughout the day.
  • Eat carbohydrates and protein before your workout.

Do NOT Train Hard Today If You Experience

  • Persistent headache
  • Dizziness when standing
  • Nausea
  • Dark urine (possible dehydration)
  • Unusual muscle weakness
  • Muscle cramping
  • Significant joint pain (especially knees, ankles, or hips)

Your Planned Workout

Chest Press

  • Use approximately 70–80% of your usual weight.
  • Leave 1–2 repetitions in reserve.
  • Do not train to failure.

Leg Extensions

  • Your quadriceps have already performed thousands of repetitions during your trek.
  • Use lighter weight than normal.
  • Focus on slow, controlled repetitions.

Leg Curls

  • Use moderate weight.
  • Perform controlled repetitions.
  • Your hamstrings may still be recovering from climbing and descending trails.

Before You Lift

  • Warm up for 5–10 minutes.
  • Perform lighter warm-up sets before working sets.
  • Stay hydrated.
  • Eat a meal or snack containing carbohydrates and protein.

Listen to Your Body

If your legs feel unusually heavy, begin cramping, or become painful during your workout, reduce the weight, shorten the workout, or skip leg exercises altogether.

If everything feels normal, gradually return to your regular training over the next few workouts.

Overall Recommendation

Since you completed a 7-day, 32-mile Philmont trek, your endurance recovery is off to a good start. Even if you don't feel particularly sore, your muscles, tendons, and connective tissues are still adapting from the sustained hiking load.

A moderate workout today is a good way to ease back into strength training. If you feel good afterward and the following day, you can resume your normal training intensity.
